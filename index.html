Notes on What Comes After

Death, despite its inevitability, is one of the hardest things people face - both going through it, and moving past it. It's fundamental. It's Natural. It's an inevitability wrought with uncertainties, like

"Why did it Happen?" "what happens next?" "Who has the right?"

It's also a definitive moment in the lives of those around it, and it isn't always bad, or good. It's an event that forces those around it to bring back the memory of what was, in all its odd and off-color forms. It's an event that forces people to decide what will come next.

This is a collection of stories from those of what's left:

The visceral memories. The tears together, the laughs, the embraces that wrap you by those of the world that’s left behind. there’s always something else; something odd, something funny, maybe. Something sad. Something small, or something big. Something about the journey, or something that makes it okay, if only for just a moment.
